Accessibility Statement

Last updated: April 14, 2026

Our Commitment

At ThriveMom, we are committed to making our website and app accessible to as many people as possible, including individuals with disabilities. We design ThriveMom to be simple, supportive, and usable in real-life moments, including when users feel overwhelmed, distracted, or cognitively overloaded. Accessibility is built into how we design, test, and improve the product.

Conformance Status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define requirements for improving accessibility for people with disabilities.

ThriveMom is substantially compliant with WCAG 2.2 Level AA.

We continuously audit and improve accessibility across the website and app. Our current efforts include:

  • Color contrast designed to meet or exceed WCAG AA ratios (4.5:1 for standard text, 3:1 for large text)
  • Touch targets designed to meet or exceed the recommended 44x44px minimum
  • Visible focus indicators for interactive elements and assistive navigation
  • Keyboard-operable interactions throughout
  • Skip links and proper landmark regions for screen reader users

While we aim for full compliance, accessibility is an ongoing process and we continue to refine and validate across devices and use cases.

Supported Environments

ThriveMom is designed to work with built-in accessibility tools on modern devices:

  • iOS: VoiceOver screen reader
  • Android: TalkBack screen reader
  • Desktop: NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver screen readers
  • Dynamic text resizing
  • Bold text
  • High-contrast display settings
  • Reduced motion settings

We use the Lexend font throughout to support readability and reduce visual strain.

We test key user flows such as joining the waitlist, browsing the FAQ, and submitting questions using these tools.

Accessibility Features and Ongoing Work

Chat Interface (App)

The chat experience is designed to work with screen readers using live regions and focus management. New messages are announced when possible, and focus is guided to relevant content.

We continue refining this behavior, especially for longer or fast-paced conversations.

Motion and Animations

ThriveMom respects your device's reduced motion settings by minimizing or disabling animations automatically.

A manual "Reduce Animations" toggle is also available in the app's Profile settings for additional control.

Color Contrast

We design text and interface elements to meet WCAG AA contrast standards across light and dark modes.

We periodically audit contrast across screens and continue improving consistency.

Touch Target Sizes

Interactive elements are designed to meet recommended minimum touch target sizes for improved usability.

We continue validating new features to maintain consistency.

If you experience an issue not listed here, please contact us. Your feedback directly shapes our improvements.

Assessment Approach

We assess accessibility through:

  • Internal testing by our development team
  • Manual testing using VoiceOver and TalkBack
  • Automated testing with axe DevTools and Lighthouse
  • Ongoing iteration based on real user feedback
